About

Bridging Science
& Engineering

Ph.D. Researcher in Mechanical Engineering working at the frontier of AI-integrated microfluidics, computational biomechanics, and intelligent flow control systems.

Currently in my third year at National Cheng Kung University (NCKU), Tainan, I investigate microfluidic platforms for zebrafish-based drug delivery, develop artificial cilia microactuation systems for on-demand particle manipulation, and apply Physics-Informed Neural Networks (PINNs) to computational hemodynamics.

My research bridges biomedical diagnostics, drug delivery, sperm activation, cognitive behavioral studies in microfluidic environments, and cardiovascular modeling — translating bioinspired engineering into clinically and industrially relevant solutions.

Before NCKU, I held a position as Junior Research Fellow at IIT Jammu (green energy & carbon capture) and as a Post-Graduate Engineering Trainee at LG Electronics India — giving me both deep academic grounding and hands-on industrial engineering experience.
